About Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, after Mercury. It is often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance.

Key facts about Mars:

  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Mass: 0.64171 × 10^24 kg
  • Moons: Phobos and Deimos
  • Orbital Period: 687 Earth days
  • Average Temperature: -63°C
  • Distance from the Sun: 227.9 million km

Geology

Mars has a diverse geological history and is characterized by vast volcanoes, deep canyons, and impact craters. The largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons, can be found on Mars.

Atmosphere

The Martian atmosphere is composed mainly of carbon dioxide, with traces of nitrogen and argon. The thin atmosphere and lack of a significant magnetic field contribute to the harsh surface conditions on Mars.
